TECHNICIAN TIPS


When performing radiographs to evaluate for collapsing trachea, taking inspiratory films of the neck and expiratory films of the chest
may help better demonstrate a dynamic collapse.


CLIENT EDUCATION


For most dogs, the tracheal collapse is managed with weight control, antitussives, and periodic administration of anti-inflammatory
drugs.
